Weasel: Out-of-Domain Generalization for Web Agents via Importance-Diversity Data Selection

Paper Code

Weasel selects a compact, diverse set of offline web-agent demonstrations for better transfer to unseen websites.

Abstract

Web agents can follow natural language goals through multi-step browser interactions, but agents trained offline on one set of websites often struggle to generalize to unseen domains. Offline training is also costly because trajectories contain long accessibility-tree states, redundant steps, and noisy demonstrations.

Weasel addresses these issues with trajectory-step selection. It scores each step for goal relevance and selects a fixed-budget subset that balances importance with diversity across states, websites, and interaction patterns. The method also uses target-centered AXTree pruning and self-reasoning synthesis for reasoning-native models.

Across AgentTrek and NNetNav training datasets, and evaluations on WebArena, WorkArena, and MiniWob, Weasel improves out-of-domain performance while producing roughly 9.7–12.5× training speed-ups over standard fine-tuning.

Method

Weasel removes noisy and less useful trajectory steps, then greedily selects a subset that is both important for the task goal and diverse enough to cover different states and actions.

Target-Centered AXTree Pruning

Long accessibility-tree states make offline web-agent training expensive. Weasel keeps a local window around the ground-truth action target, preserving the most relevant context while reducing the token budget.

Results

On WebArena-Lite with Qwen3-8B, out-of-domain AgentTrek training improves substantially when the training subset is selected with Weasel.
